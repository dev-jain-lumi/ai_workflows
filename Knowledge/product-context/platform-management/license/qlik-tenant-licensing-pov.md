# Qlik Tenant Licensing Discussion (POV)

## Context
This note captures a single discussion and point of view on licensing for HR and EM project needs under platform management.

## Option A: Two Standard Qlik Tenants (25 GB each)
- Quantity: 2 standard tenants
- Price: 6515 per year each
- Baseline yearly cost: 13030 per year

Option variants:
1. Yearly renewal with 14% markup.
2. 3-year contract (cannot stop; upgrade is possible).
3. If project lifecycle uncertainty remains (HR and EM), use 1-year license first, then decide whether to move to a 3-year contract after year 1.

## Option B: One Premium Plan
- Buy 1 Premium plan including 50 GB and support for up to 5 tenants.
- Price indication: not 2x standard, closer to 3x.
- Credon to negotiate with Qlik for a better offer.

## POV (Current Recommendation)
Two standard tenants are fit for purpose today.

Long-term view:
- Premium becomes attractive if negotiated near 2.3x to 2.4x of the 2-standard baseline.
- In absolute terms, incremental cost is around 2k to 3k per year with meaningful flexibility and AI upside.

## Premium Upside Summary
- 1000 AI questions instead of 200.
- Per-app size up to 10 GB.
- If a 3rd or 4th tenant is needed in coming years, no extra tenant licensing cost.
- Example 1: use a 3rd tenant immediately for AI projects with 1000 questions and better safety.
- Example 2: use a 4th tenant to start clean migration to CADP architecture.
- Future flexibility: if one project sunsets, GB allocation can be shifted across tenants; standard model is fixed at 25 GB per tenant.

## Decision Needed
Proceed with standard now, unless Premium is negotiated to approximately 2.3x to 2.4x and contract terms are acceptable.

- Action 2: Compare 1-year standard vs Premium total value (AI usage, expansion readiness, migration flexibility).
- Action 3: Take final buy decision after commercial proposal review.
